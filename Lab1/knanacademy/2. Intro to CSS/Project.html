Travel to mountains

Information

Hawaii's mountains are primarily massive, dormant, or active volcanoes, featuring sharp
ridges and immense elevations rising directly from the sea floor. The highest peaks are
Mauna Kea (13,803 ft) and Mauna Loa (13,679 ft) on the Big Island. Other significant
peaks include Haleakalā (Maui), Mount Wai‘ale‘ale (Kauai), and Ka'ala (Oahu).

Major Mountains in Hawaii

Of the 13 major summits of Hawaiʻi, Mauna Kea and Mauna Loa exceed 4000 meters
(13,123 feet) elevation, Haleakalā exceeds 3000 meters (9843 feet), Hualalai exceeds
2000 meters (6562 feet), and 11 peaks exceed 1000 meters (3281 feet) elevation.

  1. Mauna Kea (Big Island)
  2. Mauna Loa (Big Island)
  3. Haleakalā (Maui)
  4. Hualālai (Big Island)
  5. Mount Wai‘ale‘ale (Kauai)
  6. Ka'ala (Oahu)

Key Characteristics

  • Volcanic Origin: The islands were created by a volcanic hotspot.
  • Dramatic Topography: Known for steep, jagged ridges, deep valleys, and high prominence.
  • Climate Zones: Due to high altitude, peaks like Mauna Kea feature diverse ecosystems ranging from tropical to alpine.
